You hired your second sales rep last month. Leads are coming in from JustDial, a Google form, and three different WhatsApp numbers. Someone forgot to call a hot lead for four days and it went to a competitor. Now you're Googling "smb sales software india" at 11 PM, staring at a pricing page that wants ₹6,700 per rep/month, wondering if that's the answer.

It probably isn't. Not yet, anyway.

Most buying-guide advice for SMB sales software in India skips the only question that matters: what stage are you actually at? Buy too early and you've got a tool nobody fills in. Buy too big and you've lit ₹5 lakh a year on fire for features you'll never touch. This guide matches the tool to the pain — and to the budget you can defend.

## TL;DR

- **0–3 reps, leads still manageable:** a spreadsheet is correct. Don't buy software yet. Buying early just adds a tool reps ignore.
- **The real buy signal isn't headcount — it's pain:** leads going cold, two reps on the same lead, or nobody able to answer "who do we call right now?"
- **First purchase:** a light/India-first CRM (₹1,000–₹3,000/rep/month) if you need to *record* what happened, or a Sales Behaviour OS (₹999–₹2,999/rep/month) if you need to *change what reps do next*.
- **Do NOT buy Salesforce** below ~200 reps. It's a Ferrari for a school run — ₹6,700+/rep/month, 3–6 month setup, and 60–80% of it sits unused.
- **Match the tool to the pain, not the brand.** Re-buying when you grow up is cheap. Un-buying an enterprise contract is brutal.

## Stage 1 — 0 to 3 reps: stay on the spreadsheet

This is the part nobody selling software wants you to hear. If you have one to three reps and a few dozen open leads, a Google Sheet is genuinely the right tool. Columns for lead name, source, status, last-contacted date, next action. That's it.

A spreadsheet is free, everyone already knows how to use it, and at this volume a founder can eyeball the whole pipeline in 30 seconds. The friction of "fill in the CRM" at this stage costs you more selling time than it saves. Reps who should be making calls end up tidying records.

The spreadsheet breaks when you can no longer hold the pipeline in your head. You'll feel it before you can name it: a lead slips because the "next action" column lied, or you open the sheet and can't tell which five rows deserve a call this morning. When sorting a spreadsheet stops answering "who's about to go cold?", the spreadsheet is done.

## Stage 2 — leads going cold: the buy signal

Headcount doesn't decide when you buy. Pain does. There are three pains that mean it's time, and they tend to arrive together:

**Leads go cold and nobody notices.** A Grade A lead that gets a reply in under an hour converts at a wildly higher rate than one called the next afternoon. When inbound leads sit for two or three days because the follow-up slipped past the window, you're not losing leads to competitors — you're losing them to your own calendar. That's ₹ walking out, quietly, every week.

**Two reps call the same lead.** Or worse, both assume the other one did, and nobody calls. Duplicate effort and dropped balls are the spreadsheet telling you it can't coordinate a team.

**You can't answer "who do we call right now?"** If a founder has to open four tabs and squint to figure out the day's top five leads, the team is flying blind. Reps default to calling whoever's freshest in memory, not whoever's most likely to close.

When two of these three show up, buy. Not before.

## Stage 3 — your first purchase: CRM or behaviour OS?

Here's where most guides hand-wave. There are two genuinely different categories at this stage, and they solve different jobs.

**A light, India-first CRM** is a system of record. Its job is to remember what happened: this lead, this stage, this last call, this note. Zoho CRM is the obvious incumbent; it's about ₹1,000–₹2,500 per rep/month, ships with India defaults, and handles GST, multiple currencies, and WhatsApp integrations out of the box. Buy a CRM when your core problem is "we keep forgetting what we already knew about a lead."

**A Sales Behaviour OS** is a different animal. It doesn't just record what happened — it tells reps what to do next. Leadkaun grades every lead A–F in under 500ms, runs a Priority Queue that re-ranks in real time so the top of the list is always the call worth making, and a Missed Opportunity Engine that surfaces the ₹ at risk when a lead is about to slip. A Morning Brief lands at 8:30 AM IST so each rep starts the day knowing the five calls that matter. Pricing is ₹999 (Starter) / ₹1,999 (Growth) / ₹2,999 (Scale) per rep/month. Setup is 60 minutes; it imports straight from your CSV or Google Sheet. Buy this when your problem isn't "we forget" — it's "we know, but reps still call the wrong leads in the wrong order."

The honest distinction: a CRM changes what you *know*. A behaviour OS changes what reps *do*. If your reps are diligent but disorganised, the OS pays back faster. If they're disciplined but forgetful, a CRM may be enough. Plenty of teams run both — the CRM as the filing cabinet, the behaviour OS as the thing that actually moves the day. ## Stage 4 — when NOT to buy Salesforce

Salesforce is a great product. For an Indian SMB under roughly 200 reps, it's the wrong one — and the most expensive mistake on this list.

The list price runs ₹6,700+ per rep/month before you add Inbox, Einstein, or higher tiers. For a 10-rep team, that's over ₹8 lakh a year in licences alone, before a 3–6 month implementation and a dedicated admin salary. Compare that to ₹1,999/rep/month for a behaviour OS — about ₹2.4 lakh a year for the same team. The delta is over ₹5.6 lakh a year, every year, for features built for a 200-rep multi-region enterprise you are not.

And the cost isn't even the worst part. Salesforce's interface is built for a full-time ops user, not a field rep with 30 minutes between meetings and a WhatsApp tab open. When logging a call takes six clicks and a drop-down, reps don't log calls. Adoption at a typical Indian SMB lands at 20–40%. You pay enterprise prices for a database that's 60% empty.

Salesforce earns its keep at 200+ reps, in regulated industries (BFSI, pharma), or across multi-region multi-language operations with a dedicated admin. If that's not you, "buying to grow into it" means buying a tool you'll spend three years half-using. Buy for your stage. Re-buying when you genuinely scale costs a CSV export and a few weeks. Un-buying a signed enterprise contract costs a renewal cycle and a lot of sunk fees.

## A simple rule for matching tool to pain

Strip away the brand names and it's almost embarrassingly simple:

- **Can a founder hold the whole pipeline in their head?** Spreadsheet. Spend ₹0.
- **Are you forgetting what you already knew about leads?** Light CRM. ₹1,000–₹3,000/rep/month.
- **Do reps know the facts but still work the wrong leads in the wrong order?** Sales Behaviour OS. ₹999–₹2,999/rep/month.
- **Are you 200+ reps, regulated, multi-region, with a dedicated admin?** Now Salesforce is rational.

The mistake almost every Indian SMB makes is buying one stage too far ahead — paying for tomorrow's tool with today's budget and ending up with shelfware. The opposite mistake, clinging to the spreadsheet six months past its limit, is quieter but costs you in cold leads. Both are avoidable once you've named the pain honestly.
