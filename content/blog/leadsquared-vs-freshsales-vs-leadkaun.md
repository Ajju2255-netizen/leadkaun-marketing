If you've shortlisted LeadSquared and Freshsales, you're doing the right thing — both are real, India-viable CRMs, and both beat paying Salesforce prices. But they answer slightly different questions, and there's a third option that answers a question neither was built for. Here's the honest comparison, in India context.

## TL;DR

- **LeadSquared** — India-built, deep workflow customisation, strongest in **EdTech and BFSI**. Powerful, but configuration takes **weeks** and it leans activity-heavy.
- **Freshsales** — clean UI, fast setup, built-in **phone and email**. But **Freddy AI scoring is opaque** and not India-calibrated.
- **Leadkaun** — not a like-for-like CRM; a **Sales Behaviour OS**. Transparent A–F scoring, real-time priority queue, ₹-at-risk visibility. Built to change what reps *do*, not just store what they did.
- Pick by **job-to-be-done**: vertical workflow (LeadSquared), clean general CRM (Freshsales), or sales behaviour and rep adoption (Leadkaun).

## The one-line version of each

- **LeadSquared** is what you buy when your vertical has a high-volume, multi-stage funnel — admissions counsellors working 300 leads, insurance branches with renewal cycles — and you want to model that workflow precisely.
- **Freshsales** is what you buy when you want a clean, modern, general-purpose CRM that a small team can adopt without a consultant, with calling and email built in.
- **Leadkaun** is what you buy when the problem isn't *recording* sales work — it's getting reps to do the *right* work next, and giving managers ₹-denominated visibility into what's slipping.

## Head-to-head

### Pricing (India context)
All three are affordable next to Salesforce (₹7,500+/seat) and HubSpot Professional (~₹8,000/seat).

- **LeadSquared** — tiered; typically ₹1,500–₹4,000/seat/month with vertical add-ons.
- **Freshsales** — tiered; ₹1,500–₹4,000/seat/month depending on Growth/Pro/Enterprise.
- **Leadkaun** — ₹999 (Starter) / ₹1,999 (Growth) / ₹2,999 (Scale) per seat/month. See [pricing](/pricing).

The real cost gap is **time-to-value**, not sticker price (below).

### Setup time
- **LeadSquared** — 2–8 weeks. The power is in configuration, which means configuration is required.
- **Freshsales** — days. Sensible defaults, quick to start.
- **Leadkaun** — ~60 minutes, with Indian B2B SMB defaults (ICP weights, stages, grade thresholds) baked in.

For an SMB without a dedicated ops hire, weeks-to-value is a real cost: it's a quarter where the tool isn't earning.

### Lead scoring
This is where the three diverge most.

- **LeadSquared** — rule-based scoring you build and maintain. Flexible, but only as good as the rules someone keeps current.
- **Freshsales** — Freddy AI scoring. Convenient, but a **black box** — reps can't see *why* a lead scored high, and it isn't tuned for Indian buying behaviour.
- **Leadkaun** — transparent, three-dimensional: **Fit** (ICP match), **Intent** (engagement, with time-decay), and **Quality** (data reliability) combine into a **Grade A–F** you can explain in one sentence. Reps trust scores they understand, and trust drives adoption. More on the [scoring engine](/features/lead-scoring).

### Rep adoption
The metric that decides whether any CRM pays off.

- **LeadSquared / Freshsales** — adoption depends on how much logging you demand. The more fields, the less reps log.
- **Leadkaun** — designed around low-friction reality: **3-tap WhatsApp logging**, a [priority queue](/features/priority-queue) that removes the "who do I call next" decision, and [WhatsApp tracking](/features/whatsapp-tracking) treating WhatsApp as a first-class channel — because 70% of Indian B2B leads first-contact there.

### WhatsApp
- **LeadSquared / Freshsales** — WhatsApp via integrations/add-ons, generally bolted on.
- **Leadkaun** — WhatsApp is a native, first-class signal, not an afterthought.

### The ₹-at-risk view
- **LeadSquared / Freshsales** — report on activity and pipeline; you compute "what's slipping" yourself.
- **Leadkaun** — the [Missed Opportunity Engine](/features/missed-opportunity-engine) surfaces missed revenue in rupees automatically, and the Morning Brief lands the day's ₹ at risk in every rep's inbox at 8:30 AM IST.

## So which should you pick?

- **Pick LeadSquared** if you run a high-volume vertical funnel (EdTech admissions, BFSI renewals), have someone to own configuration, and want to model that workflow precisely. Compare directly on [Leadkaun vs LeadSquared](/compare/leadkaun-vs-leadsquared).
- **Pick Freshsales** if you want a clean, fast, general-purpose CRM with built-in calling and don't mind opaque scoring. Compare on [Leadkaun vs Freshsales](/compare/leadkaun-vs-freshsales).
- **Pick Leadkaun** if your real problem is rep behaviour and visibility — leads going cold, reps calling whomever they feel like, and managers without a ₹ number for what's at risk. It often runs *alongside* LeadSquared or Freshsales for 60–90 days before any consolidation decision.

## The pragmatic move

You don't have to bet the quarter on one tool. The lowest-risk play for most Indian SMBs:

1. Keep (or trial) the CRM that fits your workflow — LeadSquared for heavy verticals, Freshsales for clean general-purpose.
2. Add Leadkaun as the behaviour layer for 60–90 days.
3. Measure rep adoption, response time, and ₹ recovered.
4. Consolidate onto whichever earns its seat.

The tools that survive that test aren't the ones with the longest feature list — they're the ones reps actually use.
